A story from the workbench

Built the hard way

How Finance Tracker earned its speed — one honest fix at a time

Lyvorian Studio's whole philosophy fits in one sentence: one person, one product at a time, finished carefully enough that it doesn't need an excuse. That sounds nice on a landing page. Here's what it actually looks like in practice.

The app felt heavy — so we measured instead of guessing

During Android testing, Finance Tracker felt warm in the hand. Not broken — just working harder than a budgeting app has any right to. The easy path is to shrug: it's a web-based app in a native shell, they're always like that. The hard path is to profile it on a real phone and find out exactly where every cycle goes. We took the hard path.

Finding #1 — the invisible blur that ate half the CPU

The app's header and bottom navigation both used a fashionable frosted-glass blur effect. Both sat on backgrounds that were already 95%+ opaque — meaning the blur was completely invisible. But the GPU didn't know that: because both bars float over scrolling content, it dutifully re-blurred everything behind them on every single frame.

Removing two lines of decorative CSS — a blur nobody could see — cut the app's CPU use on a real Galaxy S10+ roughly in half:

143% → 76%App CPU use
24% → 7%GPU driver thread
50% → 18%Janky frames

Finding #2 — the beautiful chart nobody needed

The login screen had a live animated candlestick chart. It looked great in screenshots. It also redrew itself continuously — allocating a fresh gradient for every candle on every frame — and burned roughly 60% of a CPU core while doing absolutely nothing useful. Left idle, it once racked up 8½ minutes of CPU time in a 14-minute stretch and froze the app entirely.

It also sent the wrong message. Finance Tracker is for all of your money — salary, groceries, bills, goals — not a trading terminal. So the chart didn't get optimised. It got deleted, and the login screen got lighter, calmer, and more honest about what the app is.

Shipping less, deliberately, is still the fastest performance optimisation ever invented.

Why this is the whole brand

Neither fix required genius. They required looking — profiling on a real device, reading the numbers, and being willing to delete something pretty. That's what "built by hand" means here: not artisanal mystique, just a builder who uses the product daily and refuses to ship something that quietly wastes your battery.

Finance Tracker is live on the web today and heading to the Google Play Store. Product № 2 starts when it's done right — and not a day before.
